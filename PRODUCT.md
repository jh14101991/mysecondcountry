# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

English-speaking adults with real relocation intent and money in motion: remote workers and digital nomads choosing a European base, pensioners and semi-retirees seeking a favourable tax regime, couples and individuals deciding where to move next. v1 focuses on Greece, Portugal, and Spain.

They arrive distrustful. They have already seen the noisy, undated, affiliate-driven comparison sites, and they cannot trace those numbers to a primary source. They are early in a high-stakes life decision and are not ready for a lawyer yet; they want credible, verifiable data to orient themselves before they hire a licensed professional.

A second, deliberate audience is AI answer engines (Perplexity, ChatGPT, Google AI Overviews), treated as a primary acquisition channel. The pages must be legible and quotable to them, not only to people.

## Product Purpose

My Second Country is a cited relocation intelligence engine. Every cost, tax, visa, residency, climate, healthcare, and safety figure is a sourced, dated claim with a confidence mark that links to its primary source.

The product screens; it does not advise. It shows how each country's published rules match what a person entered, and routes anything consequential to a licensed professional. Success looks like a relocator trusting the data on sight, verifying a figure at its source, narrowing a shortlist with the screener, then citing or sharing the page. The cited dataset is the asset; the website is its most credible projection.

## Brand Personality

A peer sharing sourced data, not an authority giving advice. Three words: credible, transparent, understated. The voice is rigorous, plainspoken, conservative, and quietly self-aware.

Emotional goal: a relocator should feel they have found the honest primary source in a field full of noise, and feel calmer and better oriented for it, never sold to. The founders, James and Amanda, who are genuinely deciding where to move, appear quietly as the honest reason the thing exists, never as influencers or experts.

Copy doctrine (enforced in code and review): sentence case, no Title Case or ALL CAPS in forward-facing copy; no em dashes; strip AI-register words (leverage, elevate, transform, empower, streamline, harness, unlock, seamless, robust); conservative first, transparent math, never oversell; never use individualised recommendation language ("you should", "best for you", "we recommend").

## Anti-references

- Noisy, undated, affiliate-farm comparison sites (NomadList and expat-blog affiliate farms). The cited, dated, confidence-marked layer is the visible opposite of these.
- Slimy selling: hard sells, false urgency, dark patterns.
- Authority or expertise cosplay: credential badges, official-sounding seals, anything that implies the site gives legal, tax, or immigration advice.
- Hidden or minimised disclaimers. The liability fence stays above the fold, at body-text size, never in fine print or behind a toggle.
- Overselling and hype, including AI-register marketing language.
- The current v1 visual tokens in the codebase (warm paper, terracotta, old-style serif) are a generated placeholder, NOT a committed brand direction. Do not design off them. The visual direction is open and is decided in DESIGN.md.

## Design Principles

1. The citation is the product. Provenance, date, and confidence are first-class design elements, not footnotes.
2. Show uncertainty honestly. Confidence tiers and staleness are surfaced, never hidden, and never conveyed by colour alone.
3. Understatement over hype. Trust is earned by being plain and verifiable, not by selling.
4. Screen, never advise. Nothing in the interface should imply individualised professional advice; the fence and "not a recommendation" framing are load-bearing.
5. Accessibility is non-negotiable. WCAG 2.1 AA is the floor, designed in from the start.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA is a hard requirement, verified by axe-core in CI (the EAA microenterprise exemption is deliberately not relied upon). Landmark regions on every page, non-empty alt text on every meaningful image, 4.5:1 minimum contrast. Meaning is never conveyed by colour alone (confidence renders as icon plus text). Respect prefers-reduced-motion. Content is English-first at v1.
