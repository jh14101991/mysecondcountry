# Page roadmap, the cited backlog

A prioritized, demand-led backlog of page and topic candidates for My Second Country. This is a reference doc (not a process doc; does not count against the four-doc cap) and a menu, not a build contract. Pull from the top in order, but let search and AI-crawler logs vote on what gets depth next.

Canonical strategy lives in `STRATEGY.md`, `PRODUCT.md`, and the v1 design spec; this file is the backlog those imply.

## How this was built

Generated 2026-06-25 by a creative-ideation workflow: 12 page-family generators (each routed to a named method, Jobs-to-Be-Done, First Principles, SCAMPER, or de Bono lateral provocations, with the refuse-first-three discipline), then deterministic dedup, three completeness critics, a gap-fill round, adversarial scoring that killed slop, and an Opus synthesis that ranked the survivors. 12 families produced 216 raw candidates, scored down to 135 kept, ranked into 135 backlog items.

Ranking method: priority = intent + aeo + affiliateScore + citeability (each 1 to 5), penalized for medium or high effort and fence risk, with a deliberate thumb on the scale for the four wedge families (freshness, dealbreaker, AEO Q&A, tax-regime) and anything that reuses the live Greece 7% machinery. Ties break toward citeable-today, low-effort, low-fence-risk.

## Families, and why each earns its place

- **freshness**: Stale-data corrections (NHR closure, golden-visa tier changes, in-country DNV abolition) are the purest AEO wins and directly extend the regime wedge: dated, cited, single-fact pages incumbents have not updated.
- **dealbreaker**: The proven 'what would disqualify you' hero format, ported to every major regime and visa; high intent, high AEO, fence-safe by construction because the page screens out rather than advises in.
- **aeo-qa**: Direct question-shaped pages answering the exact strings AI engines get asked; clean single cited fact with a verifiedDate beats stale affiliate content for citation.
- **tax-regime**: The literal extension of the live Greece 7% page to the other named regimes (5A, 5C, IFICI, Beckham); each is one high-intent rule with a primary statutory source and a strong adviser referral.
- **money-arbitrage**: Cross-border tax-treaty and wealth-tax pages for the highest-affiliate-value HNW and US-person audiences; citeable to stable treaty text and statute, fence managed by stating the rule not the verdict.
- **comparison**: Multi-regime and multi-country side-by-sides that AI engines want as a single cited answer; extends the live /compare format, non-thin only when the disqualifiers and eligibility nuance carry the page.
- **decision-tools**: Eligibility, residency-test, and citizenship-clock pages anchored to a named statute or GLOBALCIT entry; high citeability, early-funnel for every downstream regime page.
- **constraint-persona**: Named-persona income-floor and pension pages (EUR 3k remote worker, UK retiree, non-EU couple) that apply cited thresholds mechanically; strong intent, watch the gross/net and discretionary-floor traps.
- **cost-housing**: Property transaction, annual property tax, and buying-eligibility pages with exact statutory percentages; high citeability and affiliate value, but mostly medium effort and second-wave to the regime/visa core.
- **practical**: Healthcare-access, social-security, and schooling pages that convert to health-insurer and lawyer referrals; useful breadth but lower intent/affiliate than the tax and visa core, so mostly next/later.
- **place-depth**: Town and island pages that hang a golden-visa zone or regional tax fact on a named place; real but thinner data (low-confidence rent tiles), so demand-led later unless the cited zone fact is decisive.

## Method notes, caveats, and sourcing honesty

This is a demand-led menu, not a build contract. Pull from the top tranche in order, but let search and AI-crawler logs vote on what gets depth next: a "next" page that starts pulling Perplexity/AI-Overview citations or affiliate clicks should jump the queue, and a "now" page that does not earn crawler attention should not be force-built.

Ranking method: priority = intent + aeo + affiliateScore + citeability (max 20), with a penalty for medium/high effort and medium/high fenceRisk, and a deliberate thumb on the scale for the four wedge families (freshness, dealbreaker, aeo-qa, tax-regime) and anything that reuses the live Greece 7% machinery. Ties broken toward (a) citeable today from existing gr/pt/es data, (b) low effort, (c) low fence risk.

Why these 15 are the top tranche:
- They are the spine of the proven wedge. Six are freshness/dealbreaker pages that are pure cited-fact corrections of stale incumbent content (the EUR 250k golden-visa floor, NHR closure, the 5 Feb 2026 consular-only DNV rule, Spain golden-visa abolition, the three disqualifier pages). These are the highest AEO-yield, lowest-effort, lowest-fence-risk pages on the board.
- Four extend the live regime page directly into the other named regimes the site already has data for: Greece 5A, Portugal IFICI, the Greece 5B year-15 exit, and the three-regime comparison. The 5B exit-year page and the 7% disqualifier page are built almost entirely from CitedValues already in greece-foreign-pensioner-flat-tax.json, so they are near-zero new sourcing.
- Two are the highest-affiliate cross-border tax pages that are still citeable today (Beckham EUR 600k ceiling, UK-Portugal pension treaty) and serve the money-in-motion HNW and UK-retiree clusters.
- The two AEO-Q&A entries (US citizen + Portugal NHR, Spain golden visa abolished) are direct question-string captures with verifiedDate 2026 facts already in the data layer.

Deliberately held out of the top 15 despite high raw scores: several near-duplicate IFICI and Spain-DNV-floor candidates (the candidate set contains multiple overlapping IFICI, Beckham, and DNV-income pages).
