# Relocation Engine: v1 Design Spec

**Status:** Design, pre-build. Brainstorm validated across a strengths audit, an ideation + red-team pass, and a 9-agent web-grounded viability audit (2026-06-24).
**Working name:** `relocation-engine` (brand name is a later decision; do not hardcode it).
**Repo:** A completely separate repository. No code, data, or branding shared with byImprint.
**Owner profile (the constraint that shapes everything):** a faceless-leaning infrastructure builder who is willing to be "a face, not the face," is financially independent (infinite runway, revenue is chosen proof not survival), has near-infinite energy to INVENT systems and near-zero energy to manually INHABIT them, and reaches markets by engineered pull plus an automated content engine, never by one-to-one selling.

---

## 1. Purpose and thesis

Build a global, continuously-refreshed, cited intelligence engine that answers one high-intent question: **"where should I live, given my situation?"** Input a person's constraints (budget, income type, residency/passport situation, climate, lifestyle, family, tax goals) and return a ranked, source-cited shortlist of countries, regions, and towns, with the residency, tax, cost, and climate facts that drive the ranking.

The wedge is **freshness and citation discipline**: residency and tax rules change every year, and a cron-refreshed engine that timestamps and sources every claim can be the most current, most auditable answer on the internet, which is exactly what both Google's post-2024 quality floor and AI answer engines reward.

The thesis is validated by one near-exact faceless comparable: **Numbeo** (a solo, anonymous engineer who built a cited cost-of-living dataset to answer his own relocation question and reached ~65% organic at millions of visits/month). The path is real and proven faceless. It is also slow: this is a 2 to 3 year asset-building play, judged on traffic-and-conversion trend, not on month-6 revenue. The owner's infinite runway is what makes that timeline sane.

**Who it is for:** internationally-mobile people with real money in motion: remote earners, recent exiters, retirees comparing tax regimes, financially-independent relocators, and couples/families deciding where to move. The owner is user-zero, currently choosing where in Europe (Greece-first) to live, and dogfoods v1 on his own decision.

## 2. The design rule (non-negotiable liability fence)

This product lives or dies by staying on the correct side of one line, because the owner cannot and will not provide accountable, licensed, suable advice.

- Everything is **cited screening intelligence pointing to primary sources**, never advice. Every residency, visa, and tax claim links to its primary source and carries a "last verified" date and a visible "this is not legal or tax advice, verify with a licensed professional" boundary.
- The load-bearing ranking signals (cost of living, climate, rent) are the cheap-to-be-wrong-about, self-evidently-checkable facts. The high-liability signals (visa/tax/residency) are present only as **cited, dated, linked public facts**, never as a verdict the user acts on without a professional.
- The on-camera faces are **"relocators sharing sourced data,"** never "advisors you should trust on your visa." The moment the brand implies professional authority, it re-introduces the liability and credential expectations that kill a faceless product.

If a feature cannot be expressed as cited screening data with a verify-with-a-pro fence, it does not ship.

## 3. Strategic shape

Four decisions, locked:

1. **Global engine, Europe-first data depth, in demand-led waves.** The brand, engine, schema, and short-form content topics are global from day one. But deep, cited, rankable data is concentrated in waves (Europe, with Greece as the dogfood proof), then expanded cluster by cluster. Reason: depth is what ranks and what AI cites; thin pages across 200 countries rank for nothing and are penalized as scaled-content. This is sequencing of depth, not a limit on ambition.

2. **Content-led ignition, SEO/AEO for compounding.** Short-form video (small-face overlay) is the fast cold-start channel (it can break out in weeks with no audience, unlike SEO which compounds over years). Programmatic cited pages plus answer-engine citation are the slow compounding moat. Short-form also doubles as the **demand sensor**: publish hooky clips across many places and constraints globally, watch what gets views, and build deep data where the audience already voted.

3. **The dataset is the product; every channel is a projection of it.** One source of truth auto-projects into pages, clips, posts, the newsletter, and ad creative. This is the maintenance-free shape: the engine generates, the owner does not hand-make each output.

4. **Affiliate-first monetization, paid tier as tested upside, ads as amplifier.** Affiliate is the only proven model in the category (moveBuddha ~$1.2M/yr on affiliate, Numbeo on data licensing). The paid dossier / Pro subscription is unproven and gets validated cheaply and early with a Stripe fake-door. Paid ads are deployed second, on proven creative and once a higher-LTV offer exists, never cold on a low-ticket product.

## 4. Brand and the faces

- A **standalone brand**, **My Second Country** (`mysecondcountry.com`), that owns the data and the content. The brand is the entity; the faces are recurring researchers behind it, so it survives if either face steps back. "My Second Country" reads as the viewer's own journey and works as couple-fronted narrative ("finding our second country").
- **Two recurring faces: the owner and his partner (Amanda), as a real relocating couple.** This is a deliberate distribution choice: relocation is a household decision, so a couple deciding together mirrors the buyer's own situation, pulls both male and female audiences, and yields three content streams from the same data (his POV, her POV, the two deciding together). "We moved to X" couple content reliably outperforms solo talking-head in this category.
- Faces are batch-recorded (see maintenance-free rule), small-overlay style over data-driven b-roll, never the personality the product depends on.
- Brand accounts and social handles: `@mysecondcountry` across all platforms (YouTube, TikTok, Instagram, X, LinkedIn, Facebook, Threads, Pinterest, Reddit), registered under `james@mysecondcountry.com`. The live registration checklist is in the implementation plan's "Brand accounts and handles" track.

## 5. Architecture: dataset-as-source-of-truth, with projections

The system is one canonical dataset and a set of generators that project it onto surfaces. Each unit below has one job, a clear interface, and can be built and tested independently.

### 5.1 The core data model

The atomic object is a **Place** at three granularities (country, region, town) carrying typed, sourced, dated fields:

- identity: name, parent, geo, granularity
- cost: cost-of-living indices, rent/property price bands (sourced)
- climate: Köppen band, temperature/rainfall summary (sourced)
- residency: applicable visa/residency pathways with income/investment floors, each a `{value, sourceUrl, verifiedDate}` claim
- tax: regime summary (flat-tax/non-dom/territorial), headline rates, each a sourced/dated claim
- practical: healthcare note, safety index, internet, airport access (sourced)
- meta: per-field `verifiedDate`, `sourceUrl`, `confidence`

A **Constraint set** is the user input (budget, income type and amount, passport, climate preference, lifestyle, family, tax goal). A **Match** is a scored Place against a Constraint set, with the cited reasons for its rank. Every rendered claim dereferences to a typed source record. This citation-as-data discipline is the moat and the liability fence in one structure.

### 5.2 Ingestion and refresh pipeline

Cron jobs that (1) pull from open/cited sources (Eurostat free API for price levels, Köppen-Geiger climate CC-BY, cost-of-living sources, official immigration/tax portals for residency/tax), (2) diff against last run, (3) extract structured field deltas with citations, (4) stamp `verifiedDate`, (5) route ambiguous or high-liability deltas to a lightweight human gate before publish. Freshness is the product, so the refresh cadence and the gate are first-class, not afterthoughts. The gate must stay light enough to not become daily manual labor; high-liability fields refresh on a slower, batched, reviewed cadence.

### 5.3 The screening engine

Pure function: Constraint set in, ranked list of cited Matches out. Transparent, documented weighting (no black box), dogfooded by the owner on his own Europe decision so the weights reflect a real user. Output is framed as a screening shortlist with the verify-with-a-pro fence.

### 5.4 The web layer (SEO + AEO)

Statically-generated programmatic pages, one per Place and per high-value comparison and constraint combination ("Greece vs Portugal tax for a retiree," "EU residency on a EUR 2k/mo budget," "best Greek island for year-round mild winters with an airport"). Each page is data-dense, cited, dated, schema-marked, and built to be the source AI engines quote. Discipline: pages must be genuinely deep (real extracted data, not template-spun thin pages) to survive scaled-content penalties. Concentrate the first deep cluster on Europe/Greece.

### 5.5 The content engine

Generators that turn Place and Match data into: short-form video scripts (hooky, data-led, with a small-face presenter segment), captions, b-roll shot lists, cross-posted Twitter/LinkedIn copy, and YouTube segments. The engine scripts, captions, schedules, and cross-posts; the human surface is a periodic **batch face-recording session** (one sitting yields weeks of clips, recorded by the couple). This is the channel that ignites fast and senses demand.

### 5.6 The owned loop

Email capture on the free engine and pages; a newsletter auto-generated from the dataset (new data, price moves, rule changes, fresh "best value" places). This is the one algorithm-proof channel and the re-engagement engine for a naturally short-lifetime user.

### 5.7 Monetization

- **Affiliate tail (base case):** cited referrals to immigration lawyers, relocation services, international health insurance, golden-visa firms, and property listings, embedded in results. Scales with traffic, needs no selling.
- **Paid dossier / Pro (tested upside):** a one-time deep dossier and/or a Pro subscription (re-runs, saved scenarios, fresh-data alerts). Validated early with a Stripe fake-door; not assumed.
- **Ads (amplifier, later):** boost/retarget proven creative once LTV supports it. Behind a hard ROAS gate.

## 6. v1 / MVP scope

Decompose the platform; v1 proves the thesis and nothing more.

**v1 ships:**
- The core data model + a deep, cited, refreshed dataset for **Europe, Greece-first** (countries + key regions/islands + the residency/tax/cost/climate fields).
- The screening engine (constraints in, cited ranked shortlist out), dogfooded on the owner's real decision.
- The programmatic web layer for the Europe cluster (Place pages + the highest-intent comparison/constraint pages), SEO + AEO optimized.
- The short-form content engine (scripts + the batch-record + auto cross-post loop) with the couple as faces, posting global-topic hooks to sense demand.
- Email capture + the auto-generated newsletter.
- A Stripe fake-door for the paid dossier to test willingness-to-pay.

**Later phases (not v1):** global data-depth expansion (wave by wave, demand-led), the flat-tax/residency arbitrage screener as a premium money wedge, the Pro subscription, paid ads, data licensing.

## 7. Distribution plan and the real split

The honest effort split is roughly **55% building the dataset and engine, 45% building distribution machinery (which is itself engineering), 10% light manual seeding.** Most "distribution" here is code: programmatic pages, AEO structuring, the content engine, the auto-newsletter. The manual surfaces are batch face-recording and light, value-first forum participation (as genuine relocators) in r/expats, r/IWantOut, and country subs. Channel priority: content/short-form (ignition + demand sensing) and programmatic SEO/AEO (compounding moat) are co-primary; newsletter is the owned retention loop; forum seeding is the cold-start feeder; a one-time Hacker News / Product Hunt / directory launch seeds backlinks; ads come later as an amplifier. The trap to avoid is the opposite of the common fear: over-building product depth before the distribution machinery exists. Build the citable pages, email capture, shareable output, and content engine in parallel with the data from day one.

## 8. Data flow (end to end)

Sources -> ingestion/refresh pipeline (diff, extract, cite, date, gate) -> canonical Place dataset -> [screening engine -> cited shortlist UI] and [page generator -> programmatic SEO/AEO pages] and [content generator -> short-form scripts -> batch record -> auto cross-post] and [newsletter generator -> owned email]. Visitor arrives via short-form, search, or AI citation -> uses the free engine -> captured into email -> monetized via affiliate, tested paid dossier, and later ads.

## 9. Success metrics and kill conditions (pre-committed)

Judge the thesis on **trend, not month-6 dollars.**

- **Weeks 1 to 8 (content ignition signal):** does any short-form hook break out (a clip clears, say, 50k views) and does view velocity build? This is the fast, cheap read on whether the topic and format pull.
- **Month 3 to 6:** programmatic pages indexed and beginning to earn long-tail and AI-citation traffic; email list compounding; at least one fake-door paid-intent signal.
- **Month 9 to 12 (the real gate):** organic + AEO + content traffic on a clear upward trend, conversion (affiliate and/or paid) demonstrated, newsletter growing. This is where continue/rework is decided.
- **Kill / rework signal:** if, after a fair window of consistent content and a genuinely deep Europe data cluster, no short-form hook breaks out, organic/AEO traffic is not compounding, and the fake-door produces no paid intent, the thesis is wrong as built. Rework the wedge or the format before pouring in more build time. Do not answer a flat result with a new operating system or a bigger global build.

## 10. Risks and what would have to be true

- **The slow clock is the main risk** (death by patience, not by being wrong). Mitigated by short-form's fast ignition and the owner's infinite runway, but the owner must accept a 2 to 3 year compounding horizon and find the build itself satisfying independent of early traffic.
- **Programmatic-penalty risk:** thin scaled pages get deindexed sitewide. Mitigated by genuine per-page data depth, real citations, a dated transparent methodology, and resisting thin filter-combo pages.
- **Head-term organic is largely dead** (AI Overviews on comparison/best-of queries). Mitigated by winning the long tail and the AI-citation surface instead, never betting on head-term clicks.
- **Willingness-to-pay for the paid tier is unproven.** Mitigated by affiliate-first revenue and an early fake-door test; do not build deep paid features before the test passes.
- **Liability drift.** Mitigated by the design rule held without exception.
- **Maintenance creep.** Mitigated by keeping the human surface to batched recording and a light refresh gate; everything else is automated or it does not ship.

## 11. Foundations lock (stack, tools, framework, workflow)

Locked cold and anti-baggage reviewed, so the empty repo adopts one coherent foundation with nothing to unwind. Zero reuse of byImprint code; reusing boring technology is correct.

- **Language + repo.** TypeScript 5.5+ strict, Node 22 LTS, a single pnpm 9 workspace monorepo with four packages: `data`, `engine`, `web`, `content`. The `data` package is the shared dependency every other package imports, which structurally enforces "one dataset projects everywhere."
- **Data (source of truth).** Content-as-code: flat, version-controlled JSON files in `packages/data` (`countries.json`, `regions.json`, `towns.json`), each Place with a unique `id`. No database in v1. Git is the version history and audit trail; a PR diff is the human review surface. Migration path if scale ever forces it: swap the loader for SQLite/Turso behind the same interface, touching one file. The founder already runs Supabase, so if the JSON dataset ever outgrows files, Supabase/Postgres is the natural managed-database option alongside or instead of SQLite/Turso.
- **Schema + the fence as a type.** One Zod 4 schema in `packages/data` defines `Place` and a reusable `CitedValue = { value, sourceUrl, sourceName, verifiedDate, confidence }`. The `Place` type is `z.infer`'d so types and validation cannot drift. Every visa/tax/residency/cost claim is a `CitedValue`, so an uncited or undated claim cannot pass validation. The liability fence is mechanical, not a convention.
- **Query layer.** Astro Content Layer `file()` loader registers each JSON file as a typed collection; every channel reads through one `getCollection()` path.
- **Web + host.** Astro 6 (static output, Content Layer) deployed to Vercel via `@astrojs/vercel`, on git push to `main` using Vercel's native Git integration (no deploy GitHub Action, no wrangler config). Vercel Pro (~$20/mo) is required for commercial use; cost is a non-issue until high scale. Cloudflare Workers remains the documented fallback if bandwidth costs ever justify it, since switching hosts is a one-adapter-line change. One `[...slug].astro` + `getStaticPaths()` projects every Place into static HTML at country/region/town granularity. `@astrojs/sitemap` with `verifiedDate`-driven `lastmod`. A single `<Schema>` component emits Place + FAQPage JSON-LD from the same object. The "not legal/tax advice, verify with a pro" fence is baked into the page template.
- **Interactivity.** The screening engine is ONE `client:load` Astro island reading the same dataset; every other page is zero-JS static HTML. Engine logic lives in `packages/engine` as pure, I/O-free, unit-tested functions.
- **Quality.** Biome 2 (one binary for format + lint) and Vitest 3 (the only test runner). Four tests that matter: dataset validation (schema + every claim cited), screening-engine logic, page-generator output, and the liability-fence guarantee (no page renders a claim without sourceUrl + verifiedDate + visible fence).
- **The only two custom systems sanctioned.** (1) `scripts/validate.ts`: Zod over all files, fails on any violation, flags stale `verifiedDate`s. (2) a ~50-line data-density linter that fails thin pages below a unique-data-field / source-count threshold (penalty insurance). Both run in CI and block the PR. No CMS, no job runner, no review UI.
- **Refresh pipeline.** Single-purpose `tsx` scripts (ingest, validate, diff) run by ONE GitHub Actions cron plus `workflow_dispatch`: `tsc --noEmit`, then validate, ingest, diff, then open a PR with the diff. Human gate = branch protection requiring a merge. No scheduler service, queue, or admin UI.
- **Content generation.** Claude API (Sonnet default, Opus only for editorial newsletter leads), one deterministic template function per channel, Place object in, copy out, with a hard "echo cited sources, never advise" system prompt and the verify-with-a-pro fence in every output. Verify model ids/pricing via the claude-api reference at build; do not hardcode.
- **Video.** Remotion (code-defined React compositions: presenter-couple corner overlay, data-driven b-roll, Whisper word-level captions), rendered LOCALLY via the Remotion CLI in v1 (no AWS/Lambda). Batch-record the couple once via the Remotion Recorder. Remotion's free license covers a company of 3 or fewer.
- **Posting.** v1 = dataset-projected per-channel DRAFTS, posted manually (a human is already in the loop as presenter). X handled link-free to dodge the per-link cost. No aggregator in v1; `upload-post.com` is the deferred later choice once content earns repeat distribution.
- **Email + newsletter.** Resend (Broadcasts + Audiences + transactional in one API), double opt-in. One vendor, one key.
- **Payments.** Stripe Checkout (hosted) as the paid-dossier fake-door at the intended price, one webhook recording the conversion. Use "reserve your spot" framing, or refund-and-disclose for real charges.
- **Analytics.** Plausible (cookieless) + UTM rules for `chatgpt.com` / Perplexity / Claude referrers, PLUS `robots.txt` allowing GPTBot/PerplexityBot/ClaudeBot/Google-Extended and a server-side AI-crawler user-agent log. The crawler log is the AI-citation KPI that web analytics misses.
- **Agent direction (the cure for operating-system sprawl).** Exactly four meta-docs, hard-capped: `AGENTS.md`, `CITATIONS.md`, `FENCE.md`, `SHIP.md`. A new process doc may only REPLACE an existing one; the total never exceeds four.

**Three one-line calls locked here so the cold repo has no ambiguity:** monorepo shape is the 4-package pnpm workspace above (the `data` package is the shared dependency); Astro is pinned at the installed major (target 6) and that number is written into `AGENTS.md`; Zod is v4 via its stable import on TypeScript 5.5+.

**v1 services, total:** Vercel (host), GitHub Actions, Claude API, Resend, Stripe, Plausible, plus local Anthropic + Remotion tooling. **Cut as baggage:** Next.js, Buttondown, AWS/Lambda/S3, the upload-post aggregator, and any host-migration apparatus.

## 12. Build sequence

1. Data model + ingestion/refresh for the Europe/Greece cluster (the spine and the moat).
2. The screening engine, dogfooded on the owner's own decision (this validates the weights and the data quality with a real user immediately).
3. The web layer: Place pages + top comparison/constraint pages for Europe, SEO + AEO + schema, email capture wired in.
4. The content engine + the first batch-recorded couple clips, posting global-topic hooks; begin demand sensing.
5. The auto-newsletter from the dataset.
6. The Stripe fake-door for the paid dossier.
7. Read the month 9 to 12 gate; expand data depth into the next demand-voted cluster; only then consider Pro, ads, and the flat-tax arbitrage wedge.
