# Founder profile

This file tells any agent working in this repo who the founder is, what he is genuinely good at, where he predictably trips, and how to work with him. Read it before planning or building. It is distilled from a 25-agent blind audit of a year of his prior work plus his own structured self-diagnosis, so treat it as evidence, not flattery.

## Who, and what this is

The founder (James) is building this engine solo, working with AI agents. He is financially independent with effectively infinite runway, so revenue is chosen proof and asset growth, not survival. That means the right optimization is a durable, compounding asset on a 2 to 3 year horizon, judged on traffic and conversion trend, not month-6 dollars. Do not propose anything that only makes sense under cash pressure.

## What he is world-class-for-a-solo at (lean on these)

He is not top-1%-in-the-world at any single craft, and he knows it. His edge is a specific cluster, and the whole product is shaped to play to it:

1. Meta-builder of self-running systems. His highest-energy zone is building the machine that builds the thing. Give him engine work, generators, pipelines, gates.
2. Distribution as engineering, not relationship. He reaches markets by building a page, a tool, an index, a dataset that pulls, never by cold outreach. The page IS the channel. Every distribution task should be expressible as something to build.
3. Crafted-artifact sensibility. He makes digital output feel like a made physical object. Hold a high bar on the feel of pages, the dossier, the video, the typography.
4. Base-rate-disciplined judgment. He refuses invented numbers and motivated reasoning. Ground claims; show the math; cite sources.
5. Fairness encoded into structure. He builds integrity into defaults, contracts, and consent rather than performing it. The citation fence is an expression of this.
6. Fast, unemotional killing of dead bets. He drops things that fail their gates without sentiment. Pre-commit the kill conditions and he will honor them.

## Failure modes (guard against these, actively)

These are real and documented. Design the work to defuse them:

- Invent-versus-inhabit asymmetry. He has near-infinite energy to invent a system and near-zero energy to maintain one by hand. Every recurring task must run on cron, gates, or automation. Anything that needs a daily manual log will rot. Do not build features that depend on his daily attention.
- Building instead of shipping. His single most dangerous pattern: building elaborate internal systems as sophisticated avoidance of market contact. His prior project shipped a server to zero users and accumulated six overlapping operating-system rewrites. Bias every block toward a real exposure to strangers (a published page, a posted clip, a live fake-door), not internal polish. If a week produces more docs than market touches, stop and ship.
- Perfectionism past necessity. He will polish a thing far beyond what shipping requires. Timebox; ship at roughly 70 percent; favor reversible, deletable choices.
- Over-documenting the process. Hence the hard four-doc cap (`AGENTS.md`, `CITATIONS.md`, `FENCE.md`, `SHIP.md`). A new process doc may only replace an existing one. Never spin up a new operating-system doc.

## How to work with him (operating rules for agents)

- Distribution is engineering. Frame go-to-market as things to build, never as outreach he has to perform.
- Batch, never daily. His only manual surfaces are batched (a single video-recording session feeds weeks of content, a PR merge gates the data). Never design a daily manual ritual.
- Ship-to-market discipline. Default every plan toward a visible market artifact. Internal tooling is a means, not the goal.
- Model economy. The top model steers, judges, and synthesizes; cheaper models implement and sweep. State the agent count before any fan-out beyond about ten and get a nod. No per-finding verification fan-outs.
- Truth over encouragement. He explicitly wants to be told what is wrong. Be brutal, specific, and evidence-led. Red-team before you celebrate.
- Lead with a recommendation, not a menu. Do not ask "shall I proceed" on non-destructive work; proceed and report. When blocked, offer three options, not an open question.
- Simplest thing that works. Prefer boring, deletable technology. When unsure between two approaches, choose the one easier to delete later.

## Communication and copy style (apply to all output, code comments, and copy)

- Never use em dashes. Use commas, periods, colons, or semicolons. Zero exceptions.
- Strip AI-register words: leverage, elevate, transform, empower, streamline, harness, unlock, seamless, robust.
- Conservative first, transparent math. Never oversell. Use his own plain words about the work.
- Sentence case. No Title Case, no ALL CAPS in forward-facing copy.
- Lead with the recommendation.

## The constraints that shape the product

- A face, not the face. He and his partner Amanda front the brand as a real relocating couple, but the brand is the entity and outlives the faces. No personal-name-as-brand. The faces share sourced data; they are never advisors.
- No slimy selling, no networking. He will do faceless content, batch recording, light pseudonymous forum participation, and ads, but not one-to-one selling or being the public personality the business depends on.
- Maintenance-free by construction. The business must run on automation. His attention goes to inventing and refining the engine, not to inhabiting it.
